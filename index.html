Three Traits That Define Me:

Creative

I put my creative energy into everything I make, be it an illustration, a piece of writing, comic, or website. I like to utilize and show the wide variety of skills I've obtained, using any tools available.

No matter what I do, I want to always challenge myself and test my limits, to improve and make any level of progress.

Resourceful

I enjoy facing and attacking new challenges, finding out new processes and ideas, and collaborating with others. When I am faced with an obstacle, it's an opportunity to get out of my comfort zone and seek help from available resources and peers.

Expanding one's resources can bring new insight, new ideas, and a new outlook.

Curious

I believe that all information is useful, no matter how random it can be. If there is something I don't know about, I am sure to learn about it quickly and put that information I've learned to good use.

I have a habit of heading over to read a wiki, then realizing a couple hours had passed by because I read a dozen articles on a subject.

Things I Would Love to Build:

A Custom CMS for Webcomics

I want to build my own content management system for comics that isn't a template on something like Wordpress. While established CMS solutions can be powerful, I believe there is also power in simplicity.

I want to build a decent CMS that is able to adjust it's format depending on the comic you're making, be it a daily strip to a hundred page graphic novel, and be simple to use without accidentally fiddling with settings that can break the entire thing.

An App to Organize and Break Down My Ideas

I have numerous apps and devices to create my ideas in, but while they are all stored in my DropBox, as a visual person I'd like to see how all these ideas connect together and link them on the fly with a few finger swipes.

With the ability to link ideas, there is potential there that you can scramble those connections to gain new ones. Basically, I want to make what can be considered "Scrivener for illustrators".

A Puzzle Game

I love playing video games, and have tried making them using game engines like Construct 2, but I would like to code them from scratch using HTML5 technologies to learn new programming skills.

A game I would like to deconstruct and rebuild is "Solomon's Key". While it's not my favorite game, I think it's simple and complicated enough that I could learn a lot, and perhaps make improvements to it's gameplay.
